On Emergence
⬡ Claim ✦ Idea ? Question
⌦

Overview

⌦

Emergence refers to phenomena where complex systems exhibit properties or behaviors that their individual components do not possess in isolation. The whole becomes, in a very real sense, greater than the sum of its parts.

⌦
Key Definition
Emergent properties arise at a level of complexity above that of their constituent elements. They cannot be predicted or reduced to the behavior of individual parts.
⌦

Strong vs. Weak Emergence

⌦

claimWeak emergence describes properties that could theoretically be derived from lower-level descriptions, given sufficient computational power. Most physical phenomena fall into this category.

⌦

src?Strong emergence holds that some higher-level phenomena are genuinely irreducible — consciousness being the most contested example. The hard problem may represent a true case of strong emergence.

Examples in Nature

⌦

Murmuration in starlings produces fluid, coordinated movement from simple local rules. No individual bird choreographs the flock; the pattern is a property of the collective interaction.

What are the main philosophical objections to strong emergence?
✦ Grove AI
Three main objections:

1. Causal closure — if physics is causally closed, emergent properties can't add new causal power

2. The explanatory gap — emergence describes without explaining why higher-level properties arise

3. Historical reduction — science has repeatedly reduced "irreducible" phenomena
